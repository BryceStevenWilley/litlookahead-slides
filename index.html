The Assembly Line Project

  • March 2020 - Now
  • 200+ volunteers building 30 guided interviews for self represented litigants
The Assembly Line project started at the beginning of the pandemic, in March 2020. The best way to illustrate what the project has done is with a story. (story here) I joined the Assembly Line at the end of 2020, and the project had been very successful before then! But looking forward, it would need to change a few things to work in a post COVID world.

Improvements: E-filing

  • long-term email filing is burdensome for courts
  • Same guided interview UI, but:
    • puts info directly into court's case management system
    • gets info from courts's system to help users file into existing cases
The first of those improvements had to do with e-filing.

E-filing Status

  • Louisiana: ready for Jefferson Parish
  • Illinois: paperwork being signed
  • Future: MA Appeals Court, parts of CA
Currently, our e-filing system is ready in Jefferson Parish, Louisiana, paper work is being signed as we speak for Illinois, and we're working to bring this technology to the Massachusetts Appeals court, and parts of California.

Automating the Tedium

  • It's hard to get 200+ volunteers: most only have 1 or 2 forms people
  • tools to remove the tedious tasks those volunteers did

Discovering: Form Explorer

  • Look at an entire jurisdiction's forms in bulk
  • Currently in alpha: https://suffolklitlab.org/form-explorer
  • Beta with more states coming soon, thanks to work by:
    • Pavlos Rousoglou
    • Lera Somova
The first of these tools is the Form Explorer, which lets you look at all of the existing forms in an entire jurisdiction, all at once to get insights about how your forms work together, and if some of your forms are a little too similar.

Prototyping: Weaver

  • turn those forms into ready-to-edit guided interview drafts in one click
The backbone of the Assembly Line has been the Weaver, which lets you turn those PDF or DOCX forms into a guided interview that you can run right away.

Refining: LIT Styler

  • get actionable feedback on where to improve your interview
  • prototype out now; alpha in a few months
Finally, we've started working on what we're calling the LIT Styler. It gives you actionable feedback, like spell checks and readability scores, to improve your interview.
